# Band 9 Linguistic Markers

## Idiomatic Expressions
- **A blessing in disguise:** Something good that seemed bad initially.
- **Bite the bullet:** To endure a painful experience or face a difficult necessity.
- **Burn the midnight oil:** To work late into the night.
- **Cost an arm and a leg:** Very expensive.
- **Once in a blue moon:** Very rarely.
- **Over the moon:** Extremely happy.
- **Through thick and thin:** Under all circumstances, no matter how difficult.
- **Under the weather:** Feeling slightly ill.

## Collocations
- **Paramount importance:** Very important.
- **Cutting-edge technology:** Most advanced technology.
- **Lifelong learning:** Continuous education throughout life.
- **Sustainable practices:** Eco-friendly habits.
- **Stark contrast:** A very obvious difference.
- **Mitigate problems:** Reduce the impact of issues.
- **Broaden horizons:** Expand one's knowledge or experience.

## Cohesive Devices (Linking Words)
- **Furthermore / Moreover / In addition:** To add information.
- **However / Nevertheless / On the other hand:** To show contrast.
- **Consequently / Therefore / Thus:** To show result.
- **For instance / To illustrate:** To give examples.
- **All things considered / To put it simply:** To summarize.
- **I must admit / Generally speaking:** To frame a personal opinion.
- **Having said that:** To qualify a previous statement.
