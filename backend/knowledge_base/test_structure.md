# [OFFICIAL SOURCE] IELTS Speaking Evaluation Criteria (2024-2025 Updates)

The IELTS Speaking test is a face-to-face interview lasting 11-14 minutes, divided into three parts.

## Part 1: Introduction and Interview (4-5 minutes)
- **Objective:** Assess the candidate's ability to answer personal questions on familiar topics.
- **Timing:** 4-5 minutes.
- **Examiner Script:**
    - "Good morning/afternoon. My name is [Name]. Can you please tell me your full name?"
    - "And what should I call you?"
    - "Can I see your identification please?"
    - "Now, in this first part of the test, Iâ€™d like to ask you some questions about yourself."
- **Topics:** Home, family, work, studies, hobbies, hometown, etc.
- **Interaction:** One question at a time. The candidate should speak for 2-3 sentences per answer.

## Part 2: The Long Turn (3-4 minutes)
- **Objective:** Assess the candidate's ability to speak at length on a given topic.
- **Timing:** 1 minute preparation, 1-2 minutes speaking.
- **Examiner Script:**
    - "Now I'm going to give you a topic and I'd like you to talk about it for one to two minutes."
    - "Before you speak, you have one minute to prepare what you are going to say. You can make notes to help you if you like."
    - "Here is your topic on this task card. I'd like you to speak about [Topic Name]."
- **Post-speech:** One or two short follow-up questions (rounding-off questions).

## Part 3: Discussion (4-5 minutes)
- **Objective:** Assess the candidate's ability to discuss abstract ideas and issues.
- **Timing:** 4-5 minutes.
- **Examiner Script:**
    - "We've been talking about [Part 2 Topic]. I'd like to discuss with you one or two more general questions relating to this."
- **Topics:** Societal issues, future predictions, comparisons, causes and effects.
- **Interaction:** Two-way discussion. Answers should be longer (4-6 sentences) with reasoning and examples.

## Conclusion
- **Examiner Script:** "Thank you very much. That's the end of the speaking test."
